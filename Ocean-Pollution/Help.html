A Summary

To reduce plastic, sediment, and urban pollution, cities and individuals can adopt a range of effective strategies involving legislation, infrastructure changes, community engagement, and adopting clean technologies.


Ways to Reduce Plastic Pollution

  • Reduce single-use plastics by switching to reusable alternatives, supporting product stewardship schemes, and advocating for bans or levies on disposable items.

  • Participate in community clean-up events and support local recycling programmes to prevent plastics from entering waterways.

  • Choose products with minimal packaging, support innovation in biodegradable materials, and dispose of plastics correctly to keep them out of waste streams.

Ways to Reduce Sediment Pollution

  • Implement erosion control techniques at construction sites, such as silt fences, stabilizing exposed soil, and planting cover crops.

  • Restore riparian zones by planting native vegetation along riverbanks, which traps sediments and stabilizes banks to prevent runoff.

  • Enforce sediment management plans for land developments and tighter regulations to keep sediment from entering rivers and harbours.

Ways to Reduce Urban Pollution

  • Adopt copper-free brake pads and increase street sweeping to reduce metals and particulates in runoff.

  • Use silt fences, sediment basins, and other engineering solutions to capture construction and urban runoff prior to water entering streams.

  • Install special drains at petrol stations and industrial sites to divert contaminated water to sanitary sewers instead of stormwater drains.
